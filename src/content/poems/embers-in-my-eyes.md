I do not glitter anymore
I smoulder.
Not in silver,
but in a fevered undertow.
There is no borrowed light in me now,
no easy shine to claim,
only a hush of heat behind my eyes,
softly whispering your name.

Where there was once a careless sparkle,
there are embers, low and deep,
red as a vow I could not keep,
bright as a wound that I chose to seep.

Tell me, is this sorrow I am wearing,
or a ritual I began?
Have I been burned by love's undoing,
or did I walk into the fire by plan?

For sometimes it does not feel like pain,
it feels like longing's slow desire,
a secret wish to be unmade,
to rise or get destroyed in the pyre I made.
